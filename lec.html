Celestial Bodies Overview

Sun

Star • Yellow Dwarf

A nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma at the center of our solar system, driving weather, seasons, and making life on Earth possible.

Mercury

Terrestrial Planet • 1st from Sun

The smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It has a solid, cratered surface heavily influenced by space weather.

Venus

Terrestrial Planet • 2nd from Sun

Earth's "sister planet" characterized by a thick, toxic atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Terrestrial Planet • 3rd from Sun

Our home planet. It is a water-rich world and currently the only known celestial body in the universe to harbor life.

Mars

Terrestrial Planet • 4th from Sun

Known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface. It is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere.

Jupiter

Gas Giant • 5th from Sun

The largest planet in our solar system. It is a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot, a storm that has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Gas Giant • 6th from Sun

Distinguished by its massive, complex, and beautiful ring system made primarily of ice particles, rocky debris, and dust.

Uranus

Ice Giant • 7th from Sun

An ice giant that rotates completely on its side. It is composed primarily of a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials above a small rocky core.

Neptune

Ice Giant • 8th from Sun

The farthest known planet from the Sun. It is dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, acting as the solar system's other ice giant.

Pluto

Dwarf Planet • Kuiper Belt

Once classified as the ninth planet, Pluto is a complex world of frozen nitrogen and ice mountains. It resides in the Kuiper Belt, a vast region of icy objects beyond the orbit of Neptune.
